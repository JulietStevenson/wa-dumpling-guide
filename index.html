I've always had a love for dumplings since I was little. It was a tradition for every Chinese New Year, my chinese teacher would invite my classmates and familes over the night of Chinese New Year for a dumpling making celebration. She would have the pork filling prepared along with other traditional chinese snacks. We would wet the edges of the dumpling wrapers and place a spoonful of pork filling. Our teacher would then teach us how to properly fold the dumplings so none of the filling would expload while they were being boiled. After making about 10 dumplings each, she would prepare the dumplings while us kids would go into the livingroom and dance around to traditional chinese music until the dumplings were ready.

It wasn't until I moved to Seattle recently when I rekindled my love for dumplings. Seattle is an amazing place to find different types of dumplings, so I was determined to find the best restaurant for them. My ideal dumpling would be pan fried or fried to perfection where you get that crispy bottom (hence "Crispy Bottoms") and juicy inside. My boyfriend and I even tried to make our own dumplings one time. They were actually very easy to make and we had fun trying to make different shapes out of them. Some of them were more difficult then others but in the end, they still tasted amazing.
